Here's a pure data-driven comparison.

Hands-On Test Results

I used the same prompt — "A golden retriever running along the beach at sunset" — across all five tools, generating three videos each. Here's what I found:

Most generous credits: Kling AI 65 credits per day, enough for 3–5 short videos (~10 credits for 5s, ~20 credits for 10s). Daily reset means you never "run out" permanently. Image quality is above average, though occasional hand deformities occur.

Most consistent quality: Runway Despite only 125 credits (one-time), the visual quality of each video noticeably exceeds other tools. The downside: once credits are gone, you have to pay.

Fastest generation: Hailuo AI Generation speed crushes the competition — a 5-second video typically completes in under 10 seconds. Image quality is average, but speed is its advantage. 66–100 daily credits are sufficient for casual use.

Cheapest paid upgrade: Pika At $8/month for substantial credits, it's the best value for frequent generators. The free tier's 10 daily generations are adequate for light use.

Most capable but most expensive: Sora Longest video length (20 seconds), best overall quality, but requires a ChatGPT Plus subscription ($20/month). No standalone free tier.

Lessons Learned: Common Pitfalls

Pitfall #1: Runway's 125 credits are one-time only I assumed Runway offered 125 credits per day — it's actually a one-time allocation. I burned through all 5 videos on day one and the credits were gone. Pace yourself.

Pitfall #2: Kling queues are brutal during peak hours Weekday evenings (8–11 PM) see heavy congestion, with Kling generation queues reaching 10–15 minutes. Weekends are worse. Use during off-peak hours.

Pitfall #3: All free versions have watermarks Every free tier includes watermarks, just in different positions and sizes. For commercial or professional use, you'll need to pay for watermark removal.

Pitfall #4: Video length is capped Free versions max out at 4–10 seconds. Want a 30-second video? You'll need to pay or stitch multiple clips together.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use free AI videos commercially? Most tools allow it, but with watermarks and terms-of-service restrictions. Kling and Hailuo permit non-commercial use on free tiers; Runway and Pika require paid plans for commercial use.

Do free versions have watermarks? Yes. All five tools add watermarks to free-tier videos. Kling's watermark is in the bottom-right corner, Hailuo's in the bottom-left, and Runway and Pika's in the bottom-right.

Which AI video tool has the best quality? Runway and Sora deliver the best quality, followed by Kling. Hailuo and Pika offer decent quality with faster generation speeds.

Do free credits reset daily? Kling, Hailuo, and Pika reset daily. Runway's 125 credits are a one-time allocation. Sora uses ChatGPT Plus credits.

How do I remove watermarks from free versions? You pay. There is currently no free watermark removal method. Kling at ¥9.9/month is the cheapest option for watermark-free videos.

Can AI video tools create long videos? Free versions cap out at 10 seconds. For longer videos, you'll need to generate multiple clips and stitch them together, or upgrade to a paid plan. Sora's paid tier supports up to 20 seconds.

FAQ

Which free AI video tool has the most credits in 2026?

Hailuo AI (MiniMax) offers 66-100 credits daily plus 300 signup bonus. Kling AI gives 66 credits/day. Both reset daily.

Can I use free AI-generated videos commercially?

No. All major AI video tools restrict commercial use on free tiers. A paid subscription is required for commercial rights.

Do free AI videos have watermarks?

Kling has watermarks (removable with paid plan). Hailuo sometimes has no watermark. Luma has watermarks. Runway and Pika free tiers include watermarks.

How long can free AI videos be?

Free tiers typically generate 5-10 second clips. Kling supports up to 10s, Hailuo 5-6s, Luma 5s. Longer videos require paid plans.

Which AI video tools work in China?

Kling AI and Hailuo AI are directly accessible in China. Runway, Pika, Sora, and Luma require VPN access.

Is there a big gap between free and paid versions?

Yes. Paid versions remove watermarks, offer higher resolution, longer durations, commercial rights, and priority queuing. Free tiers are best for trying out tools and personal projects.
