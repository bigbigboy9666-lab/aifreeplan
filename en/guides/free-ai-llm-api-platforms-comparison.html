🥇 Tier 1: Most Generous Free Tiers

1. SiliconFlow — The King of Free Tiers

SiliconFlow is currently the most generous free AI API platform. The free tier offers RPM 500 and TPM 2 million in ultra-high concurrency, supporting DeepSeek, Qwen, GLM, and multiple open-source Chinese models. No VPN needed, direct access from China, extremely developer-friendly.

Free tier details:

  • RPM (requests per minute): 500
  • TPM (tokens per minute): 2 million
  • Free models: Filter by free-text-model tag (GLM-Z1-9B, etc.)
  • Commercial use: Allowed
  • Paid pricing: From ¥0.006/1K tokens

Best for: High-frequency calls, China-direct access needs, scenarios requiring multiple model switching. RPM 500 is unmatched among free APIs.

2. Groq — The Fastest Free API

Groq uses custom LPU (Tensor Processing Unit) chips to deliver inference speeds far beyond competitors. The free tier offers 1000 requests per day, with RPM 30 and TPM 30K. While the quota is not the largest, the speed advantage is significant.

Free tier details:

  • Daily free requests: 1000
  • RPM: 30
  • TPM: 30,000
  • Supported models: Llama, Mixtral, Gemini, and more
  • Pricing: Pay-as-you-go

Best for: Latency-sensitive applications, real-time conversation systems, production environments requiring fast responses.

3. GitHub Models — Free GPT-4.1 Access

GitHub Models is Microsoft's free AI inference service. You only need a GitHub account — no credit card required. The free tier supports GPT-4.1, GPT-4o, and other OpenAI models with 150 daily requests.

Free tier details:

  • Daily free requests: 150
  • RPM: 15
  • Supported models: GPT-4.1, GPT-4o, and others
  • Credit card required: No
  • Pricing: None (forever free)

Best for: Personal developers learning GPT-series models, prototype development, scenarios not requiring high-frequency calls.

4. NotebookLM — Google's Completely Free AI Tool

NotebookLM is Google's AI note-taking tool, completely free. Upload PDFs and documents as knowledge bases, and let Gemini answer questions based on your materials. While it does not offer a traditional API, it is fully free as an AI tool.

Free tier details:

  • Usage: Completely free
  • Supported file types: PDF, Google Docs, and more
  • Underlying model: Google Gemini
  • VPN needed: Yes (for most regions outside US)

5. Cline.bot API — Forever Free

Cline.bot API offers permanent free usage with a 1 million token context window and zero credit consumption. A relatively new platform with an aggressive free strategy.

Free tier details:

  • Free model: Forever free, zero credit consumption
  • Context window: 1 million tokens
  • Signup bonus: $0.5 for paid models

📊 Key Metrics Explained

RPM (Requests Per Minute)

Requests per minute. Higher values mean stronger concurrency. SiliconFlow's 500 RPM is far ahead of all free APIs, while Groq's 30 RPM is sufficient for most personal projects.

TPM (Tokens Per Minute)

Tokens per minute. Determines how much text can be processed in a single request. SiliconFlow's 2 million TPM means you can process extremely long documents in one go.

Daily/Monthly Quotas

Some platforms allocate free quotas daily or monthly, such as Groq's 1000/day and Cloudflare's 10,000 Neurons/day. These quotas typically reset at midnight UTC.

❓ FAQ

Q: Which AI API platform has the most generous free tier?
A: SiliconFlow offers the highest free tier with RPM 500 and TPM 2 million, supporting DeepSeek, Qwen, and multiple models. Groq provides 1000 requests/day, GitHub Models 150 requests/day.
Q: Can I use free APIs for commercial purposes?
A: It depends on the specific platform. SiliconFlow allows commercial use, but GitHub Models and Groq generally do not. Always review each platform's terms of service.
Q: How should Chinese users choose free AI APIs?
A: Prioritize platforms with direct China access like SiliconFlow (RPM 500), iFlytek Spark, DeepSeek, and Tongyi Qianwen. These platforms work without VPN and have fast access speeds.
Q: How to avoid being charged after free tier is exhausted?
A: Set usage alerts and spending limits in each platform's dashboard. Cloudflare and OpenRouter support budget caps to prevent unexpected charges.
Q: What is the difference between RPM and TPM?
A: RPM (Requests Per Minute) controls concurrency capacity; TPM (Tokens Per Minute) controls the processing volume per request. Together they determine the API's performance ceiling.
