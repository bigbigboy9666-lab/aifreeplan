Midjourney has no free tier — it costs $10/month minimum. But in 2026, several free AI image generators deliver 80% of Midjourney's quality. Here are the best alternatives with real free credits.

Quick Answer

Midjourney has no free credits and starts at $10/month. Free alternatives that come close: Ideogram (10 images/day free), Leonardo AI (15 images/day free), Flux (open source, unlimited local use), and Bing Image Creator (~100 generations/day). For most users, these free tools are enough.

Midjourney's business model is clear: win on quality, not on free users. V7 output is genuinely the best in the industry — aesthetics, detail control, and consistency are unmatched. But the $10/month starting price keeps many people away.

Midjourney occasionally opens free trials, but timing is unpredictable. Don't count on it.

Free Alternatives Ranked

Ideogram — Best for Text in Images

Free: 10 images/day, no watermark.

Ideogram's killer feature is text rendering. While other AI tools garble text in images, Ideogram produces clean, readable English text. Perfect for posters, logos, and social media graphics.

Free tier has resolution limits, but it's sufficient for social media use.

Leonardo AI — Most Free Credits

Free: 15 images/day, no watermark.

Leonardo AI gives you the most free images — 15 per day adds up to 450/month. Quality isn't Midjourney-level, but it's more than good enough for most purposes.

Supports multiple models including Flux. Free tier locks some advanced features, but text-to-image and image-to-image are fully available.

Flux — Open Source, Unlimited

Flux is open source and runs locally for free. If you have a decent GPU (16GB+ VRAM), use ComfyUI or WebUI to run Flux with no limits.

No GPU? Some platforms offer free Flux access:

  • Poe: Daily free credits can call Flux
  • Various community platforms

Adobe Firefly — Best for Designers

Free: 25 generations/month.

Adobe Firefly integrates deeply with Photoshop and Illustrator. If you already use Adobe tools, Firefly is the natural choice. Free credits are limited (25/month), but generated images are commercially licensed.

Bing Image Creator — Zero Barrier

Free: ~100 fast generations/day (powered by DALL-E 3).

The easiest free tool to start with. Just need a Microsoft account. Quality is average, but it's dead simple and works for casual use.

Decision Guide

Need text rendering: Ideogram Need volume: Leonardo AI (15/day) Have a good GPU: Flux (local, unlimited) Use Adobe tools: Firefly Zero setup: Bing Image Creator

Smart strategy: Use free tools to learn and experiment. Once you have a consistent need for premium quality, upgrade to Midjourney. The $10/month is worth it — but only if you actually need that level of quality.

Common Questions

❓ FAQ

Does Midjourney have free credits?

No regular free tier. Occasional trials appear but are unpredictable. Minimum $10/month.

What's the best free Midjourney alternative?

Depends on needs: Ideogram for text rendering (10/day), Leonardo AI for volume (15/day), Flux for local use (unlimited).

Can free tools match Midjourney quality?

About 80% of Midjourney quality. Flux comes closest to V6 in certain use cases.

Is Flux really free?

Open source and free, but needs a GPU with 16GB+ VRAM. No GPU? Try Flux through Poe's free credits.

Does Ideogram free tier have watermarks?

No watermarks. Ideogram's free tier gives you 10 images/day with no watermark — a major advantage.

How good is Bing Image Creator?

Average quality (DALL-E 3 powered). Not as good as Midjourney or Ideogram, but extremely easy to use with ~100 free generations daily.
