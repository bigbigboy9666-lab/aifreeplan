Home / Guides / Free Ai Translation Tools 2026

I've tested AI translation tools extensively over the past three years. The biggest lesson? "Free" doesn't always mean "free enough." Some tools cap you at 500 characters per day. Others block document translation on free plans. And some have translation quality that makes Google Translate look like a human interpreter.

This guide breaks down what you actually get for free with each tool — no marketing fluff, just the real limits I've verified.

Quick Answer: Which Free Translation Tool Should You Pick?

Before diving into details, here's the decision matrix:

  • Need unlimited free translation? → Google Translate (130+ languages, no character limit)
  • Need the best translation quality? → DeepL Free (5,000 characters per translation, 33 languages)
  • Working in WeChat ecosystem? → Tencent Translator (free, WeChat mini-program)
  • Reading academic papers? → Zhiyun Literature Translator (free, preserves PDF formatting)
  • Building an app? → Caiyun Translate API (1M characters/month free)

Now let's break down each tool's free tier in detail.

DeepL Free: Best Translation Quality, But Limited Free Tier

DeepL consistently produces the most natural-sounding translations, especially for Chinese-English and European language pairs. The catch? Its free tier is the most restrictive.

Free Tier (verified from DeepL official website, May 2026):

  • 5,000 characters per translation
  • Weekly translation session limit (requires account after hitting limit)
  • No document translation (Pro only)
  • No glossary (Pro only)
  • 33 languages supported

Paid Plans:

  • Pro Starter: ~$8.74/month (billed annually) — unlimited characters, document translation
  • Pro Advanced: ~$28.74/month — glossary, custom rules
  • Pro Ultimate: ~$45.74/month — team management

My Experience: Free DeepL is perfect for translating short texts — emails, chat messages, menu items. But translating long articles? You'll be copying and pasting every 5,000 characters. And the weekly session limit means heavy users might hit the wall by Wednesday.

Best For: Users who need high-quality translations for short texts. If you translate a few paragraphs per day, DeepL Free is unbeatable.

Fallback: When your DeepL free quota runs out, switch to Google Translate or use an AI chatbot (ChatGPT, DeepSeek) for translation — quality is surprisingly good.

Google Translate: Completely Free, 130+ Languages, No Limits

Google Translate is the most accessible translation tool on the planet. Web version and mobile app are completely free with no character limits.

Free Tier (verified from Google Translate, May 2026):

  • Web version: completely free, no character limit, 130+ languages
  • Mobile app: completely free, camera translation, voice translation, offline translation
  • API: 500,000 characters/month free ($20 per million characters after that)
  • Document translation: free, supports PDF, Word, PPT formats

My Experience: Google Translate's Chinese-English quality improved significantly in 2024-2025 (likely powered by Gemini models). It's still not as natural as DeepL for complex sentences and technical terms, but the unlimited free usage makes it the go-to choice for high-volume translation.

API Free Tier: 500,000 characters/month is generous for small projects. If you're building a multilingual app, this is the most cost-effective API option.

Best For: High-volume translation, multilingual needs, budget-conscious users. For daily browsing, travel, and casual translation, Google Translate is more than enough.

Tencent Translator: Best for WeChat Users, Completely Free

Tencent Translator is deeply integrated with the WeChat ecosystem. You can use it directly in WeChat mini-programs without installing any additional apps.

Free Tier (verified from Tencent Translator official website, May 2026):

  • Web version: completely free, no character limit
  • WeChat mini-program: free, voice translation, camera translation
  • Mobile app: free, conversation translation, simultaneous interpretation
  • API: 5 million characters/month free
  • 17 languages (fewer than Google, but covers major languages)

My Experience: Tencent Translator's Chinese-English quality is solid for everyday conversations. The WeChat mini-program integration is incredibly convenient — no app switching required. However, its lesser-known language translations aren't as good as Google Translate's.

Best For: Users in the WeChat ecosystem, cross-border e-commerce, international traders. If you work primarily in WeChat, this is the most convenient option.

Baidu Translate: Veteran Chinese Translator, Generous Free Tier

Baidu Translate has been around for over a decade and remains one of China's most popular translation tools.

Free Tier (verified from Baidu Translate Open Platform, May 2026):

  • Web version: completely free, no character limit, 200+ languages
  • Mobile app: free, voice translation, camera translation, offline translation
  • Standard API: 1,000 calls/hour, no character limit (registration required)
  • Advanced API: 5 million characters/month free (requires business verification)
  • Document translation: free, supports PDF, Word, PPT, Excel

My Experience: Baidu Translate handles general Chinese-English translation adequately, but struggles with specialized domains (medical, legal, financial). The document translation feature is a standout — free users can translate entire PDFs while preserving formatting.

Best For: Users who need document translation, lesser-known language support, or work primarily within China. Cross-border sellers find Baidu's document translation particularly useful.

Immersive Translate: Browser Extension Game-Changer

Immersive Translate is a browser extension that shows bilingual translations — original text and translation side by side — making it perfect for language learning and reading foreign content.

Free Tier (verified from Immersive Translate official website, May 2026):

  • Browser extension: free, bilingual web page translation
  • PDF translation: basic PDF translation supported
  • Video subtitle translation: YouTube and other platforms
  • Translation engines: Google Translate, DeepL, and others available
  • Pro version: ~$5/month for advanced features

My Experience: This is the best browser translation extension I've used. The bilingual display is perfect for learning English — hover over any English text to see the Chinese translation instantly. The free version is feature-rich enough for most users. The Pro version mainly adds custom translation engines and advanced PDF features.

Best For: Users who read foreign-language websites frequently, language learners, and anyone who wants bilingual content without switching tabs. Developers, researchers, and students are the biggest users.

Zhiyun Literature Translator: Academic Researchers' Best Friend

Zhiyun is specifically designed for translating academic literature. Its key feature? Preserving PDF formatting — formulas, charts, references stay intact.

Free Tier (verified from Zhiyun official website, May 2026):

  • Completely free, no limitations whatsoever
  • PDF literature translation with formatting preserved
  • Instant word translation (select text for immediate translation)
  • Multiple translation engines (Google, DeepL, Baidu — switchable)
  • Windows and Mac desktop only

My Experience: Zhiyun is my go-to tool for reading English academic papers. Select any text in a PDF, and the Chinese translation appears instantly. Translation quality depends on which engine you choose (I recommend DeepL or Google). Best of all, it's completely free with no ads and no feature restrictions.

Best For: Researchers and students who need to read English academic papers. If you frequently translate text within PDFs, Zhiyun is the best free option available.

Caiyun Translate: Most Generous API Free Tier

Caiyun Translate offers one of the most generous free API quotas among all translation tools.

Free Tier (verified from Caiyun official website, May 2026):

  • Web version: free, bilingual display
  • Mobile app: free with daily usage limits
  • API: 1 million characters/month free ($15 per million after that)
  • 6 languages (Chinese, English, Japanese, Korean, French, Spanish)

My Experience: Caiyun's API is incredibly generous — 1 million free characters per month is more than enough for most projects. The web and mobile translation quality is average compared to DeepL and Google Translate. It's mainly popular among developers using the API.

Best For: Developers who need large API quotas for multilingual applications. If you're building an app that needs translation, Caiyun's API is one of the most cost-effective options.

Youdao Translate: All-in-One Translation + Dictionary

Youdao Translate, by NetEase, combines translation with dictionary and learning features.

Free Tier (verified from Youdao official website, May 2026):

  • Web version: completely free, no character limit
  • Mobile app: free, voice translation, camera translation
  • API: 1 million characters/month free (application required)
  • 100+ languages
  • Document translation: free, supports PDF, Word, and other formats

My Experience: Youdao Translate offers comprehensive features but ranks in the middle for translation quality. The document translation feature works well and preserves formatting. The large existing user base of Youdao Dictionary makes it a convenient choice for students.

Best For: Users who want an all-in-one tool (translation + dictionary + learning). If you already use Youdao Dictionary, Youdao Translate is the most convenient option.

Lessons Learned: Don't Trust "Free" at Face Value

The biggest mistake I see people make is choosing a translation tool based on the word "free" alone. Here's what actually matters:

  1. Character limits: How many characters per translation? How many per day/month?
  2. Feature restrictions: Does the free plan support document translation? Voice translation? How many languages?
  3. Translation quality: Is the output natural? Are technical terms accurate?

For high-volume use (10,000+ characters/day), stick with Google Translate or Tencent Translator (truly unlimited free). For quality-focused use, DeepL Free is unbeatable — just work within the 5,000 character limit. For WeChat-centric workflows, Tencent Translator's mini-program is the most convenient.

❓ FAQ

Is DeepL Free enough for daily use?

If you translate short texts (emails, messages, menus) under 5,000 characters each, DeepL Free is excellent. For long articles or documents, the character limit becomes frustrating. Consider Pro Starter (~$8.74/month) for heavy use.

Does Google Translate have a free usage limit?

The web version and mobile app are completely free with no character limits. The API has a 500K character/month free tier. For personal use, you'll never need the API — the web and app versions are unlimited.

Which translation tool supports the most languages?

Google Translate supports 130+ languages, the most of any translation tool. Baidu Translate claims 200+ languages, but many lesser-known languages have poor quality. DeepL supports 33 languages with the highest quality.

Are free translation tools good enough for professional use?

For casual use (browsing, travel, chat), absolutely. For professional domains (medical, legal, technical), use DeepL or specialized translation services. AI chatbots (ChatGPT, Claude) also produce surprisingly good translations for specialized content.

What's the difference between Immersive Translate Free and Pro?

Free: bilingual web page translation, basic PDF translation, YouTube subtitle translation. Pro (~$5/month): custom translation engines, advanced PDF features, more customization options. Most users are fine with the free version.

Is Zhiyun Literature Translator really completely free?

Yes, Zhiyun is 100% free with no feature restrictions. It's designed specifically for academic literature — supporting PDF translation with formatting preservation, instant word translation, and multiple translation engines. The only limitation: desktop only (Windows and Mac).
