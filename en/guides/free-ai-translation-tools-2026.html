Home / Guides / Free Ai Translation Tools 2026

Last Friday at 11pm, I was scrambling to translate an English business proposal into Chinese. I started with DeepL — three paragraphs in, it hit me with "weekly free quota exhausted." Switched to Google Translate, which worked fine, but the output read a bit off, like it was trying too hard. I didn't wrap up until 1am. Not fun.

After that night, I decided to properly test every free AI translation tool I could find — DeepL, Google Translate, Tencent Translator, Baidu Translate, Immersive Translate, Zhiyun, Caiyun, and Youdao. Eight tools, three months of testing, plenty of lessons learned. Here's what I actually found, with data sources and verification dates for every free tier.

The Short Answer: Which One Is Actually Worth Using?

If you don't want to read the whole thing, here's what I'd tell a friend. Google Translate, Tencent Translator, and Zhiyun are truly free with no limits — use as much as you want. DeepL has the best translation quality, but the free tier caps you at 5,000 characters per translation and has weekly session limits. Immersive Translate is one of those tools where the free version is genuinely good enough for most people. Let me walk you through each one.

Once You Try DeepL, There's No Going Back — But Can the Free Tier Keep Up?

DeepL is hands down the best translation tool I've used. For Chinese-English and European language pairs, the output reads like a human translator wrote it — Google Translate doesn't even come close. The problem? Its free tier has more restrictions than any other tool on this list.

According to the DeepL official website (verified May 2026), the free version caps each translation at 5,000 characters — roughly 800 Chinese characters. That means a 3,000-word English article requires multiple copy-paste cycles. On top of that, there's a weekly session limit, and once you hit it, you need to create an account to continue. Document translation and glossary features? Those are Pro-only. It supports 33 languages, which covers the major ones but falls short compared to Google's 130+.

Pricing-wise, Pro Starter runs about $8.74/month (billed annually) for unlimited characters and document translation. Pro Advanced is ~$28.74/month and adds glossary and custom rules. Pro Ultimate is ~$45.74/month for team management features.

In practice, if you're only translating short texts — emails, chat messages, restaurant menus — DeepL Free is more than enough, and the quality is unmatched. But the moment you need to translate a long article, that 5,000-character limit becomes a real pain. Heavy users often burn through their weekly quota by Wednesday.

My advice: if you don't translate huge volumes but care about quality, DeepL Free is the best choice. For the rest of your text, fall back to Google Translate or AI chatbots like ChatGPT and DeepSeek — their translation quality is surprisingly decent.

Google Translate: The Reliable Workhorse That Never Runs Out

Google Translate is the tool I reach for most often, for one simple reason — it's completely free with no character limits, on both web and mobile. It supports over 130 languages, more than any other tool on this list. The mobile app also does camera translation (point your phone at a menu and it translates on the spot), voice translation, and offline translation — incredibly handy when traveling abroad.

According to Google Translate (verified May 2026), the web version and app are completely free with no restrictions. The API offers 500,000 free characters per month, then $20 per million after that. For most people, you'll never need the API — the web and app versions are unlimited. Document translation is also free, supporting PDF, Word, and PPT formats.

On quality, Google Translate improved noticeably in 2024-2025, likely after integrating Gemini models. Chinese-English translations are more natural than before, but still a step behind DeepL — especially for long sentences and technical jargon, where it occasionally stumbles. The trade-off is worth it though: unlimited free usage means you never have to worry about hitting a quota.

If you translate in high volumes, need lots of languages, and don't want to spend a dime, Google Translate is the safest bet. For daily browsing, travel, and casual use, it's more than good enough. Developers can also take advantage of the API — 500,000 free characters per month is plenty for small projects.

Tencent Translator: Translate Right Inside WeChat, No App Needed

The best thing about Tencent Translator is that it lives inside WeChat. You don't need to download anything — just open the WeChat mini-program and start translating. It supports voice and camera translation in the mini-program, and the standalone app adds conversation translation and simultaneous interpretation. The web version is completely free with no character limits.

According to Tencent Translator's official website (verified May 2026), it supports 17 languages — far fewer than Google Translate, but it covers all the major ones. The API is generous: 5 million free characters per month.

In my experience, Chinese-English translation quality is quite good, especially for everyday conversational text. The WeChat integration is genuinely convenient — no switching between apps. Where it falls short is lesser-known languages, which don't match Google Translate's quality. Cross-border e-commerce sellers and international traders tend to love it, since they can handle translations without ever leaving WeChat.

Baidu Translate: The Veteran With the Most Generous Free Quotas

Baidu Translate has been around for over a decade and is still going strong in 2026. The web version and app are completely free, supporting over 200 languages — even more than Google Translate, though many of the lesser-known languages aren't great quality-wise. The app supports voice translation, camera translation, and offline translation, which is useful when you're traveling without internet access.

According to Baidu Translate Open Platform (verified May 2026), the Standard API allows 1,000 calls per hour with no character limit — you just need to register. The Advanced API offers 5 million free characters per month but requires business verification. Document translation is free and supports PDF, Word, PPT, and Excel while preserving formatting.

In testing, Baidu's general Chinese-English translation is acceptable, but it struggles with specialized domains like medical, legal, and financial texts. The real star feature is document translation — the free version can handle entire PDFs without messing up the layout, which is better than many competing tools.

If you regularly translate documents, need lesser-known languages, or primarily work within China, Baidu Translate is worth trying. Its document translation feature is genuinely excellent.

Immersive Translate: Install One Extension, Never Struggle With Foreign Websites Again

Immersive Translate is a browser extension, and its core feature is bilingual display — showing the original text and translation side by side on any webpage. It might sound simple, but in practice it's a game-changer. You open an English webpage, hover over any paragraph, and a Chinese translation appears right next to it. No copy-pasting, no switching windows — the reading experience is incredibly smooth.

According to the Immersive Translate official website (verified May 2026), the free version supports bilingual web page translation, basic PDF translation, and video subtitle translation on platforms like YouTube. You can choose from multiple translation engines including Google Translate and DeepL. The Pro version is about $5/month and mainly adds custom translation engines and advanced PDF features.

Honestly, the free version is powerful enough that most users never need to upgrade. The people I know who use this extension the most are developers, researchers, and students — anyone who regularly reads English-language content. If that sounds like you, I highly recommend giving it a try.

Zhiyun: The Lifesaver for Reading Academic Papers, and It's Completely Free

If you're a graduate student or researcher, you know the pain of reading English academic papers. Packed with jargon, complex sentence structures, formulas, and charts — regular translation tools turn them into word salad. Zhiyun was built specifically for this problem. It's an academic literature translation tool that preserves PDF formatting: formulas, charts, and references all stay exactly where they should be.

According to Zhiyun's official website (verified May 2026), it's completely free with no feature restrictions. It supports PDF literature translation and instant word translation — select any text in a PDF and the Chinese translation appears alongside it. You can switch between Google, DeepL, and Baidu translation engines; I'd recommend DeepL or Google for the best quality. The only downside is it's desktop-only (Windows and Mac) — no mobile version.

Zhiyun has been my go-to for reading English papers for over two years now. Completely free, no ads, no feature restrictions — tools this generous are rare. If you regularly read English-language research, you owe it to yourself to try it.

Caiyun Translate: Developers' Favorite, With an Absurdly Generous API

Caiyun Translate is made by Caiyun Technology. You can use the web version and app for free, with bilingual text display. It supports six languages — Chinese, English, Japanese, Korean, French, and Spanish — not a huge selection, but it covers the commonly needed ones. The app has daily usage limits, though the exact quota isn't publicly disclosed.

Where Caiyun really shines is the API. According to its official website (verified May 2026), you get 1 million free characters per month, then $15 per million after that. This is one of the most generous free API quotas among all translation tools. I know several indie developers who rely on it for their projects — it's more than enough for small to medium applications.

Full disclosure: the web and app translation quality is just average — not on par with DeepL or Google Translate. Caiyun is primarily a developer tool. If you're building a multilingual app and need a translation API, Caiyun offers excellent value.

Youdao Translate: Dictionary and Translation in One Place, a Student Favorite

Youdao Translate is made by NetEase and offers comprehensive features: text translation, document translation, and web page translation across 100+ languages. The web version is completely free with no character limits. The app supports voice and camera translation. Document translation is free too, handling PDF, Word, and other formats while preserving layout. The API offers 1 million free characters per month, though you need to apply for access.

Translation quality is middle-of-the-road among the tools I tested — not the best, not the worst. Youdao's biggest advantage is its integration with Youdao Dictionary. If you're already using Youdao Dictionary to look up words, switching to Youdao Translate for full-text translation is seamless. The Youdao Dictionary user base is massive, especially among students in China.

Head-to-Head: How the Four Main Tools Stack Up

After reading all that, you might still be wondering which one to pick. Let me put the four most popular tools side by side. DeepL's free tier limits you to 5,000 characters per translation, while the other three — Google Translate, Tencent Translator, and Baidu Translate — are completely free with no limits. For language coverage, Baidu leads with 200+ languages, Google has 130+, DeepL supports 33, and Tencent has 17. In terms of Chinese-English translation quality, DeepL is the best, Google is second, and Tencent and Baidu are both "good." For API free quotas, DeepL offers nothing free, Google gives 500K characters/month, and both Tencent and Baidu offer 5 million characters/month. Document translation is free on all except DeepL's free tier.

The simple version: pick DeepL for quality, Google Translate for volume, Tencent Translator for WeChat workflows, and Baidu Translate for document translation.

A Hard Lesson: "Free" Doesn't Always Mean "Free Enough"

I once recommended a translation tool to a friend after seeing "free translation" on its homepage. A month later, my friend discovered the free version only allowed 1,000 characters per day — anything beyond that required payment. That experience taught me something important: "free" and "free enough to actually use" are two very different things.

When choosing a translation tool, focus on three things. First, character limits — how much can you translate per request, and what's the daily or monthly cap? Second, feature restrictions — does the free plan support document translation, voice translation, and how many languages? Third, translation quality — does the output sound natural, and are technical terms accurate? High-volume users should stick with Google Translate or Tencent Translator. Quality-focused users should go with DeepL. WeChat users should try Tencent Translator's mini-program. Each tool has its sweet spot.

❓ FAQ

Is DeepL Free enough for daily use?

If you translate short texts (emails, messages, menus) under 5,000 characters each, DeepL Free is excellent. For long articles or documents, the character limit becomes frustrating. Consider Pro Starter (~$8.74/month) for heavy use.

Does Google Translate have a free usage limit?

The web version and mobile app are completely free with no character limits. The API has a 500K character/month free tier. For personal use, you'll never need the API — the web and app versions are unlimited.

Which translation tool supports the most languages?

Google Translate supports 130+ languages, the most of any translation tool. Baidu Translate claims 200+ languages, but many lesser-known languages have poor quality. DeepL supports 33 languages with the highest quality.

Are free translation tools good enough for professional use?

For casual use (browsing, travel, chat), absolutely. For professional domains (medical, legal, technical), use DeepL or specialized translation services. AI chatbots (ChatGPT, Claude) also produce surprisingly good translations for specialized content.

What's the difference between Immersive Translate Free and Pro?

Free: bilingual web page translation, basic PDF translation, YouTube subtitle translation. Pro (~$5/month): custom translation engines, advanced PDF features, more customization options. Most users are fine with the free version.

Is Zhiyun Literature Translator really completely free?

Yes, Zhiyun is 100% free with no feature restrictions. It's designed specifically for academic literature — supporting PDF translation with formatting preservation, instant word translation, and multiple translation engines. The only limitation: desktop only (Windows and Mac).
