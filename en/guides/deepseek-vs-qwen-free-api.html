DeepSeek vs. Tongyi Qianwen Free API: Which Should a Regular Developer Choose?

Hello everyone, I'm your AI tool assistant. Recently, I've been helping a few friends with small projects and, yet again, got stuck on choosing a free API. These two "domestic gems"—DeepSeek and Tongyi Qianwen—both claim to offer free quotas, but what's the actual experience like? Today, let's put aside the fancy marketing and break down these two options from a developer's perspective, using the most practical data and experiences.

1. Free Quota: How Much Can You Actually Get for Free?

This is what everyone cares about most. Let's get straight to the hard comparison table:

| Feature | DeepSeek (V3) | Tongyi Qianwen (Qwen-Plus) | Comparison Conclusion |

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| Daily Free Token Quota | Approx. 1 million Tokens | Approx. 1 million Tokens | A tie; both are very generous. |

| Requests Per Second (RPS) | Officially capped at 10 | Officially capped at 10 | A tie, but the actual experience differs. |

| Model Context Length | 128K | 32K (Plus), 134K (Max) | DeepSeek's base model provides more. |

| Free Model Versions Supported | DeepSeek-V3 | Qwen-Plus, Qwen-Turbo | Both are flagship models, no downgrade. |

| Price (After Exceeding Free Quota) | Input: ¥1 / million Tokens

Key Points:

  • Quotas are basically the same: 1 million tokens per day is more than enough for building a small tool, a bot, or doing some content generation.
  • The key difference lies in "actual usability." While both cap at 10 RPS, the service stability and response speed of the two can feel different during peak hours, which we'll detail below.

    • 2. Real-World Experience: Is it Fast? Stable? Effective?

    Looking at numbers alone isn't enough; we need to see the results. I spent several days conducting stress tests with the same set of prompts (including code generation, long-text summarization, and Chinese creative writing).

    1. Speed and Stability: DeepSeek's "Speed" is Impressive

  • DeepSeek: Response speeds are generally faster, with time-to-first-token significantly shorter than Tongyi Qianwen. During peak hours (8-10 PM), while there may be occasional queuing, instances of dropped connections or failures are extremely rare. It feels like the server resources are more generously allocated.
  • Tongyi Qianwen: Speed is average, but during peak hours, response latency increases noticeably, sometimes requiring retries due to connection timeouts. In terms of stability, there are occasionally one or two inexplicable errors.
  • Analogy: DeepSeek is like a well-tuned sports sedan—quick acceleration and clear road feedback. Tongyi Qianwen is like a comfortable family SUV—smooth, but a bit slower on rapid acceleration.

    • 2. Capability and Effectiveness: Each Has Strengths, but DeepSeek is More "Well-Rounded"

  • Code Generation: Both are strong. However, DeepSeek seems more natural in understanding complex logic and generating complete function blocks with comments, with a higher success rate on the first try. Tongyi Qianwen sometimes requires more precise prompt guidance.
  • Long-Text Processing: Thanks to its 128K context length, DeepSeek has a clear advantage when processing ultra-long documents, providing more accurate summaries and Q&A. Tongyi Qianwen's 32K handles medium-to-short texts fine, but it tends to "forget" earlier content with longer inputs.
  • Chinese Creativity and Understanding: This is Tongyi Qianwen's traditional strong suit. In poetry, copywriting, and understanding complex Chinese semantics, the text it generates often has more "flavor" and a better grasp of human nuances. DeepSeek, on the other hand, leans more towards the precision and logic of a "science student."
  • Instruction Following: DeepSeek has a higher compliance rate for formats (like JSON output) and complex instructions, with a lower probability of formatting errors.

    • 3. Horizontal Comparison with Other Free APIs

  • Kimi (Moonshot): Generous free quota (128K context), strong in creative writing and information synthesis, but its code generation stability and API control granularity currently feel less stable than DeepSeek's.
  • Zhipu GLM: A veteran player with balanced capabilities, but its free quota and speed policies change frequently, giving less of a "safe for sustained free use" feeling compared to DeepSeek and Tongyi Qianwen.
  • Baidu Wenxin Yiyan (ERNIE Bot): Good ecosystem, but the free API model's version and capabilities are significantly weaker compared to the paid version, with more restrictions.

Summary: DeepSeek is like a hexagonal warrior, especially inspiring confidence in stability and speed, suitable for most general development scenarios. Tongyi Qianwen is like a humanities specialist, capable of giving you pleasant surprises in specific Chinese contexts.

3. How to Choose? Here's a Decision Tree

Stop overthinking. Match your needs to the following:

Choose DeepSeek Free API if you are:

1. An individual developer/student: Working on graduation projects or personal projects, seeking stable output without wanting to spend time debugging API connection issues.

2. A full-stack application developer: Your application needs to handle code, text, logic reasoning, and multiple other tasks simultaneously.

3. Sensitive to response speed: Building chatbots, real-time interactive tools, where 0.5 seconds faster makes a world of difference in user experience.

4. Have long-text processing needs: Such as document analysis or book summarization.

Choose Tongyi Qianwen Free API if you are:

1. A content creator: Primarily generating Chinese copy, marketing content, or creative writing, needing it to "understand Chinese better."

2. An Alibaba Cloud ecosystem user: Already using other Alibaba Cloud services (like OSS, Function Compute), making integration more convenient.

3. On an extremely tight budget: When the quota runs out, its slightly lower input price can save you a bit more.

4. Handling lightweight dialogue/Q&A: Processing relatively short but semantically complex Chinese conversations.

4. Some Final Honest Words

1. There's no "best," only "most suitable." I'm recommending DeepSeek a bit more today because its recent overall experience (speed, stability, capability) better aligns with my expectations for a reliable "productivity tool."

2. Policies can change. Free quotas and model versions may be adjusted at any time. Both companies are currently in a phase of market grab, so cherish the window for free use.

3. You must try it yourself! Don't just take my word for it. Spend half an hour using your own core use cases (e.g., "Help me write a Python web crawler with exception handling" or "Write a modern poem about involution") to call both APIs and experience them firsthand. Personal testing is the most reliable method.
