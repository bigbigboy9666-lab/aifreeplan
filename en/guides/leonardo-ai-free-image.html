15 free AI images per day, no watermarks

I tried a bunch of AI art generators recently. Midjourney has the best quality but zero free credits. Ideogram is great at text rendering but only gives you 5 images a day. Then I landed on Leonardo AI — 150 free tokens daily, enough for about 15 standard images or 6 HD ones. The free tier has no watermarks, supports the Alchemy HD engine and ControlNet for precise control. Among free AI art tools, it's genuinely the most generous option out there.

What the free tier actually gives you

150 tokens per day, each image costs 10 to 24 depending on model and resolution. Standard 512x512 runs about 10 tokens — that's 15 images per day. HD 1024x1024 costs about 24 tokens — roughly 6 per day. Credits reset automatically every day, no manual claiming needed. The free tier's selling points: no watermarks (many competitors force them), up to 1024x1024 resolution, Alchemy HD engine available, and thousands of community fine-tuned models to pick from. The downsides: free users queue behind paying customers during peak hours, some advanced features like AI Canvas and Motion require a paid plan, and commercial use needs the paid tier ($12/month+).

How it compares to Midjourney, Ideogram, and others

Free credits: Leonardo gets 15/day, Midjourney has none, Ideogram gives 5/day, Adobe Firefly offers 25/month, Bing Image Creator provides 15/day. Nobody adds watermarks. Resolution-wise Midjourney and Firefly can hit 2048, the rest cap at 1024. Model variety is where Leonardo really shines — community models plus official ones, while the others mostly stick to a single model. Overall Leonardo delivers the best free experience: most credits, no watermark, rich model selection. Midjourney wins on raw quality but has zero free tier. Ideogram excels at text rendering but only 5 images daily.

Is the Alchemy HD engine worth it

Alchemy is Leonardo's quality enhancement engine and it's available on the free tier. Turn it on and you get richer detail, more accurate colors, better composition, and higher output consistency. The trade-off is burning through more tokens faster. My strategy: use standard mode for drafts, switch to Alchemy only for the final version to maximize token efficiency.

The community model library is a goldmine

Leonardo has an active community model library where users upload fine-tuned models for every style imaginable — photorealistic portraits, anime, concept art, product design, architectural rendering. Free tier users get access to all of them, and many match official model quality. This is Leonardo's unique advantage that most people overlook.

ControlNet: a must-have for designers

Free tier supports ControlNet, letting you precisely control human pose, edge detection (Canny), depth information, and semantic segmentation. For designers this is essential — neither Midjourney nor DALL-E offers this level of control. For more AI image tools, see our Ideogram free guide.

What trips up beginners

150 tokens sounds generous until you realize HD generations eat through them fast — test with lower quality first. Model choice matters a lot too; different models produce wildly different results, so start with the default before experimenting with others.

❓ FAQ

Does Leonardo AI free tier have watermarks?

No. Free tier output has no watermarks — a major advantage over many competitors.

How many images can I generate with 150 daily tokens?

~15 images at standard resolution (512x512), ~6 at HD (1024x1024).

Can I use Leonardo AI images commercially?

Not on the free tier. Commercial use requires the paid plan ($12/month+).

Which is better: Leonardo AI or Midjourney?

Quality: Midjourney wins. Free credits: Leonardo wins. Control: Leonardo wins (ControlNet). Beginner-friendly: Leonardo wins.

Do I need a VPN for Leonardo AI?

Yes, if you're in mainland China. Leonardo AI is not available there directly.
