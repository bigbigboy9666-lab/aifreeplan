Quick Answer

AI music generation is free in 2026. Suno gives you 50 credits/day (5-10 songs), and Udio offers about 10 songs/day. Both are enough for casual use, but they differ significantly in output quality, track length, and workflow.

---

Suno vs Udio Free Tier Comparison

Suno is the most popular AI music generator. Free users get 50 credits daily. Standard mode costs 5 credits per song (up to 4 minutes), high-quality mode costs 10 credits. That's 5-10 free songs per day.

Udio was founded by former Google DeepMind researchers and launched in April 2024. Free users get approximately 10 songs per day, but each free track is capped at 30 seconds. You can extend clips using the continuation feature, but each extension costs credits.

Key difference: Suno generates complete songs (up to 4 min) in one shot. Udio generates 30-second clips that you piece together. Suno is more convenient; Udio produces higher audio quality.

---

What Can You Actually Do With Free Credits?

Casual listening: Enough. 10 songs a day adds up to 300 different tracks per month.

Short video background music: Barely enough. A 1-minute BGM takes 3-5 attempts to get right.

Serious music production: Not enough. A full song needs 20-30 iterations. Free credits only cover demo stage.

Commercial use: Neither tool allows commercial use on free plans. YouTube monetization, ads, and games require a paid subscription.

---

The Biggest Gotchas With Free Tiers

Gotcha 1: No commercial use on free plans. Suno explicitly marks free-tier songs as "non-commercial only." Udio has the same restriction. Using free-tier music in monetized YouTube videos is technically a violation.

Gotcha 2: Udio free tracks are only 30 seconds. Many users expect full songs but get half-minute clips. The extension feature works but costs additional credits.

Gotcha 3: Suno high-quality mode doubles credit cost. Standard mode: 5 credits/song. High-quality: 10 credits/song. Always-on HQ mode means only 5 songs/day instead of 10.

Gotcha 4: AI-generated lyrics have murky copyright. Neither Suno nor Udio has clear terms on lyrics ownership. Don't use AI-generated lyrics for official releases.

---

Which One Should You Choose?

Choose Suno if:

  • You want complete songs in one generation (up to 4 minutes)
  • You're a beginner — larger community, more tutorials
  • You want simple, transparent credit system
  • Better Chinese song support

    • Choose Udio if:

  • You want the highest possible audio quality
  • You need fine-grained style control with tags
  • You primarily make instrumental music
  • You're willing to piece together 30-second clips

    • My recommendation: Try both — they're free. Suno for quick demos, Udio for polished output. If you only pick one: Suno for casual use, Udio for serious music.

FAQ

FAQ

Which has more free credits: Suno or Udio?

Suno gives 50 credits/day (5-10 songs in standard mode). Udio offers ~10 songs/day but each is only 30 seconds. For complete songs, Suno wins.

Can I use free AI music commercially?

No. Both Suno and Udio explicitly prohibit commercial use on free plans. YouTube monetization, ads, and games require a paid subscription.

Why does Udio limit free tracks to 30 seconds?

It's Udio's freemium strategy — short clips showcase quality while encouraging upgrades. You can extend clips with the continuation feature, but each extension costs credits.

Is Suno high-quality mode worth it?

Audio quality is noticeably better, but it costs 10 credits per song instead of 5. Try standard mode first, then switch to HQ for final versions.

Are there completely free AI music generators?

Google MusicFX is free but requires waitlist. Stable Audio has a free tier with 20-second limits. For the best free experience, Suno's daily 50 credits are hard to beat.

Who owns the copyright to AI-generated songs?

Legal ownership is still unclear in most jurisdictions. Both Suno and Udio have vague terms. Don't use AI-generated lyrics for official releases without legal review.
