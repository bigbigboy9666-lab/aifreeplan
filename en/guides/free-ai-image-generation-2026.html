Quick Summary — This guide compares the free tiers of 15 AI image generators in 2026. Best overall: Leonardo AI (15 images/day, no watermark). Best for volume: Bing Image Creator (100/day via DALL·E 3). Best for text in images: Ideogram. Best no-VPN option for China users: Jimeng AI.

Every AI image tool claims to be "free." Most of them are lying — or at least stretching the truth. After testing 15 tools hands-on, here's what the free tiers actually look like in 2026.

Leonardo AI: The Best Free Option Overall

Leonardo AI's free tier gives you 15 images per day with no watermark and multiple model choices including Kino XL and Diffusion XL. The 720p resolution works fine for social media and blog posts.

Paid plans start at $9/month, but honestly, the free tier handles most personal use cases. I used the free version for three months before I even considered upgrading.

)

Bing Image Creator: Microsoft's Free DALL·E

Microsoft made DALL·E 3 available for free through Bing Image Creator. 100 generations per day is the highest free limit of any tool on this list.

The catch: outputs tend to have that "AI look" — overly polished, a bit generic. You also lose some control over style and composition compared to tools like Leonardo AI or Midjourney. But for blog thumbnails and social media posts, 100 free images per day is hard to beat.

Ideogram: The Only Tool That Gets Text Right

Every other AI image tool produces garbled text. Ideogram actually renders readable words — logos, headlines, signs, UI mockups. If your use case involves text in images, Ideogram is the only serious option.

Free tier has daily credit limits, but it's enough for occasional use.

Tools for China-Based Users (No VPN Needed)

If you're in China and don't want to deal with VPNs:

  • Jimeng AI (即梦AI) — 66 credits/day, fast access, good Chinese prompt understanding
  • Tongyi Wanxiang (通义万相) — Alibaba's tool, reliable free tier
  • LiblibAI — Community-driven, new users get free credits

These tools understand Chinese prompts natively, which is a real advantage since translating prompts to English often loses nuance.

Lessons Learned: Mistakes I Made

I paid $10/month for Midjourney for six months before discovering that Leonardo AI's free tier produced comparable results for my use case (social media graphics). That's $60 I didn't need to spend.

Another gotcha: "free trials" that give you 100 credits but charge 20-50 credits per generation. You end up with 2-5 images, not 100. Always check the per-generation cost, not just the total credit count.

Playground AI advertises "50 images/day" but the free tier limits you to older models. The high-quality models require a paid plan. Read the fine print.

Quality Comparison: What Each Tool Does Best

❓ FAQ

Which AI image tool has the most free credits in 2026?

Bing Image Creator leads with 100 generations/day (DALL·E 3). Playground AI offers 50/day. Leonardo AI gives 15/day with no watermark.

Do free AI image tools add watermarks?

Leonardo AI's free tier has zero watermarks — that's its key advantage. Bing Image Creator is also watermark-free. Midjourney has no free tier at all.

Which AI tool can generate images with readable text?

Ideogram is the only AI image tool that reliably renders legible text. Other tools produce garbled characters. Use Ideogram for logos and posters.

Is Midjourney free?

No. Midjourney discontinued free trials in 2023. Plans start at $10/month. For budget users, Leonardo AI's free tier is the best alternative.

Can I use AI-generated images commercially?

It depends on the tool. Adobe Firefly has the clearest commercial licensing. Leonardo AI allows commercial use on some models. Always check the specific tool's terms.

What's the best free AI image tool for beginners?

Leonardo AI — 15 free images/day, no watermark, multiple models, and an intuitive interface. Start there and explore others as needed.
