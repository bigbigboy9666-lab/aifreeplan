    • Search has changed

    I used to Google everything. Then one day I searched for a technical problem and spent three pages clicking through SEO-stuffed articles that all said the same vague thing. That's when I started looking for alternatives.

    Perplexity changed how I search. Ask a question, get a structured answer in 3 seconds, with source citations on every key claim. Click a link, verify the information yourself. No more clicking through ten pages to piece together an answer from fragments.

    Now my workflow is: Perplexity for English content and technical questions, 秘塔搜索 for Chinese content and domestic news, and Google only when neither of those works.

    Six AI search engines, tested honestly

    Perplexity: The gold standard for English search

    Perplexity is the best AI search experience available today. Free users get a few Pro searches daily (using stronger models), with unlimited standard searches.

    The killer feature is source citations. Every answer links to its sources — you can verify claims instantly. For research and fact-checking, this is invaluable.

    My experience: searched "SWE-Bench leaderboard 2026" and got a clean ranked table with every data point linked to its source. With Google, I'd need to open five or six pages to assemble the same information.

    Pro costs $20/month for unlimited Pro searches, file uploads, and image generation. The free tier works fine for most people.

    The catch: Chinese-language search quality lags behind English. When searching for domestic Chinese content, it sometimes misses relevant results from Chinese websites.

    秘塔搜索 (Metaso): Best for Chinese-language search

    Metaso is the strongest AI search engine for Chinese content. Completely free, no registration required, results include source citations, and the interface is clean and ad-free.

    Compared to Perplexity, Metaso understands Chinese content significantly better. For Chinese news, WeChat articles, and Zhihu answers, Metaso surfaces far more relevant information.

    It also has an "academic search" mode — switch to it for papers and research data, and the results are impressively high quality.

    My experience: searched for details on Doubao's pricing plans and got a comprehensive breakdown including prices, feature comparisons, and user reviews, all sourced from WeChat articles and tech media. The same search on Perplexity returned much weaker results.

    The catch: English search is weaker than Perplexity. For English-language content, stick with Perplexity.

    ChatGPT Search: Search built into the chat

    ChatGPT now includes a built-in search feature. Free users can access it, but you need to trigger it explicitly — ask a question requiring real-time information, and ChatGPT will search the web automatically.

    The advantage is follow-up capability. After getting initial results, you can ask "is this source reliable?" or "are there opposing views?" — like talking to a research assistant.

    Free tier search is limited (shares daily quota with GPT-4o), but sufficient for everyday use.

    The catch: Not all questions trigger search. Sometimes ChatGPT answers from its own knowledge without going online. You need to explicitly ask it to search.

    Phind: A search engine for developers

    Phind is purpose-built for technical questions. For programming problems, API documentation, and code examples, its answers beat general-purpose search engines.

    The free tier provides a reasonable number of daily searches. Responses include code examples with step-by-step explanations.

    The catch: Weak for general searches. For non-technical topics, Perplexity or 秘塔搜索 perform better. It's a technical search engine — don't expect it to do everything.

    You.com: A veteran with mixed results

    You.com was one of the early AI search players. The free tier offers basic AI search with source citations.

    Compared to Perplexity, You.com returns lower-quality results and responds more slowly. Its advantage is customizable search sources — you can restrict searches to specific websites.

    Who it's for: People with specific search needs (academic-only sources, specific news outlets). Regular users are better off with Perplexity or 秘塔搜索.

    Google AI Overviews: AI summaries bolted onto traditional search

    Google added AI-generated summaries above search results in 2025. Search a question, get an AI summary first, then traditional results below.

    The upside: you can get a quick answer without clicking any links. The downside: quality is hit-or-miss. Sometimes the summary is accurate; sometimes it treats advertising content as factual information.

    I don't rely on this feature because it's fundamentally an add-on to traditional search, not a true AI search experience.

    Which one should you use?

    Primarily English content: Perplexity. A few free Pro searches daily plus unlimited standard searches.

    Primarily Chinese content: 秘塔搜索. Free, no registration, best Chinese understanding.

    Developers searching technical problems: Phind. Direct code examples with explanations.

    Want to cover everything: Use Perplexity + 秘塔搜索 together for full Chinese-English coverage.

    What went wrong

    I initially thought Google AI Overviews was good enough — no need for a separate AI search engine. Then I searched for a health-related question and the AI summary treated a marketing article as factual information. I almost believed it. After that, I only trust AI search with source citations.

    Another time I used Perplexity for a very recent Chinese news story and got nothing useful. Perplexity's Chinese source coverage can't match 秘塔搜索. Switched to Metaso and found the answer instantly. Now I use different tools for different languages by default.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Q1: Will AI search engines replace Google? A: Not in the near term. Google still excels at general search, shopping, maps, and other specific scenarios. AI search is more of a complement — use it when you need quickly synthesized information, and Google when you need to find specific web pages.

    Q2: Are AI search answers accurate? A: Those with source citations (Perplexity, 秘塔搜索) are significantly more accurate because you can click through and verify. Those without citations (Google AI Overviews) are prone to errors. Build a habit of checking sources.

    Q3: Is the free tier enough? A: For most people, yes. Perplexity free gives a few Pro searches daily plus unlimited standard searches. 秘塔搜索 is completely free. If you search more than 30 times a day, you might need Perplexity Pro.

    Q4: What about privacy? Are my searches saved? A: Most AI search engines save search history to improve service. Perplexity offers a privacy mode. 秘塔搜索 claims not to save search history. Check each platform's privacy policy for details.

    Q5: How is this different from just searching in ChatGPT? A: ChatGPT Search operates within conversation context, good for follow-up questions and deep discussion. Dedicated AI search engines (Perplexity, 秘塔搜索) offer more focused search experiences, more structured answers, and clearer source attribution.
