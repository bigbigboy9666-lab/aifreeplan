    • Google quietly gutted the free tier

    On May 18, 2026, Google introduced a "weekly percentage cap" for Gemini free users. Instead of a fixed daily limit, you now get a weekly budget. Use it up, and you wait until next week.

    Peak hours got worse too. During Pacific time business hours (roughly midnight to noon Beijing time), free users are essentially locked out of Gemini 2.5 Pro. One Chinese tech blog put it bluntly: "Google has completely excluded free users from Gemini 3 Pro during peak hours."

    This is the third time Gemini's free tier has been cut since 2025:

    • Late 2025: Quietly lowered daily request caps
    • January 2026: Cut again — some users reported Pro model limits dropping to ~50 requests/day
    • May 2026: Weekly percentage caps introduced, peak hour restrictions tightened further

    Google One Basic ($1.99/month)

    • 100GB storage
    • No Gemini premium features

    Google AI Pro ($19.99/month)

    • Gemini 2.5 Pro access

    Google AI Ultra ($249/month)

    • Higher usage limits
    • Early access to new features
    • For professionals and developers

    Google AI Studio's API free tier still offers ~1,000 free requests/day (Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite), but that's for developers, not regular users.

    What the free tier can still do

    Don't panic yet. The free tier still works for basic tasks:

    Still works fine:

    • Casual chat and Q&A: Flash model handles simple tasks
    • Basic image understanding: upload a photo, get analysis
    • Short translations and writing
    • Google Search integration: this is Gemini's unique advantage — no other AI does this natively

    Starting to struggle:

    • Long document analysis: context window is limited
    • Complex reasoning and code: Flash is noticeably weaker than Pro
    • Extended conversations: hitting limits triggers throttling
    • Peak hour usage: expect queues or outright rejection

    Effectively paywalled:

    • Gemini 3 Pro: free users almost never get access
    • Veo video generation: paid only
    • Deep Research: paid only

    Is Google AI Pro worth $19.99/month?

    If you use Gemini primarily for:

    • AI-assisted writing in Gmail and Docs: this is Google's独家优势 (unique advantage) — no other tool integrates this deeply with Google Workspace
    • Gemini 2.5 Pro reasoning for code and data analysis
    • Veo video generation: other platforms charge more for comparable video AI
    • 2TB Google cloud storage: the plan doubles as a storage + AI bundle

    Then AI Pro is reasonable.

    But if you mainly chat and write articles, $19.99/month is steep. ChatGPT Plus at the same price offers a stronger base model. Claude Pro at $20/month has better long-context handling.

    Is $249/month for Ultra insane?

    For most people, yes. Ultra is aimed at developers and professionals who need Gemini 3 Pro and maximum usage limits. If you're not building on Google's platform, skip it.

    The bigger picture

    Google's move signals the end of "generous free AI." The playbook across the industry is identical:

    1. Launch free, build user base
    2. Start cutting limits quietly
    3. Introduce paid tiers
    4. Push free users toward paid with increasingly restrictive quotas

    Gemini was one of the most generous free AI tools in 2024-2025. By mid-2026, the free tier is essentially a trial that pushes you toward $19.99/month.

    The silver lining: competition keeps alternatives viable. DeepSeek, Kimi, and ChatGPT's free tiers are still strong. If one tool starts charging too much, switch to another. The AI market is fragmented enough that no single player has a monopoly on free access — yet.

    ❓ FAQ

    Is Gemini free tier completely unusable now?

    No. Basic features still work - daily chat, simple Q&A, translation are fine. Complex tasks and peak-hour experience are just worse.

    What's the difference between Google AI Studio free API and Gemini App free tier?

    AI Studio is a developer API with ~1000 free calls/day. Gemini App is the consumer chat interface. They're independent - AI Studio isn't affected by App rate limits.

    Gemini free vs DeepSeek free - which is better?

    For search, choose Gemini (Google Search integration). For Chinese chat and writing, choose DeepSeek. For code and reasoning, they're similar but DeepSeek has no usage limits.

    What does the $19.99/month AI Pro include?

    Gemini 2.5 Pro access, Veo video generation, 2TB storage, Flow video creation, Whisk image-to-video, NotebookLM enhancement, Gmail/Docs integration.

    Any tricks to bypass Gemini free tier limits?

    Avoid peak hours, use Google AI Studio free API instead of App, save Pro model for complex tasks and use Flash for simple ones.

    Will other AI tools shrink their free tiers too?

    It's already happening. Doubao started charging, Claude limits free users. The AI subsidy era is ending industry-wide.
