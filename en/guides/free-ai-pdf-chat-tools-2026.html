5 Free AI PDF Chat Tools Compared (2026): Real Limits Tested

Quick Summary: I tested 5 AI PDF chat tools to find their real free tier limits. ChatPDF gives you 2 files/day with 120 pages each. ChatDOC allows 2 files/day but only 20 pages each. Humata offers 60 pages total. NotebookLM is completely free with 50 sources per notebook. DocGPT has a free GPT-3.5 trial. Bottom line: use ChatPDF for daily quick reads, NotebookLM for deep research.

Last month I needed to extract key data from an 87-page industry report. Reading it manually would take 3 hours. With an AI PDF chat tool, I asked 15 questions and had all the answers in 10 minutes.

But the free tier differences between these tools are huge. Some let you ask 10 questions total. Others have no limits at all. I spent a week testing 5 popular tools and documented every limit. Here's what I found.

Free Tier Comparison at a Glance

  • ChatPDF: 2 PDFs/day, 120 pages each, 50 questions per file
  • ChatDOC: 2 files/day, 20 pages each, 200 questions/day
  • Humata: 60 pages total, 10 answers (one-time allowance)
  • NotebookLM: Completely free, 50 sources per notebook, no question limits
  • DocGPT: Free GPT-3.5 trial, $4.99/month for GPT-4

ChatPDF: Best for Daily Quick Reads

ChatPDF

Free

Free tier: 2 PDF uploads per day, up to 120 pages per file, 50 questions per file. Resets daily.

Paid plan: ChatPDF Plus at $15/month for unlimited files and questions.

Model: GPT-3.5 (free), GPT-4 (Plus).

ChatPDF is my go-to for quick PDF analysis. Upload a file, start asking questions. The killer feature: every answer includes page-number citations so you can verify the source instantly.

The main limitation is the 2-files-per-day cap. If you need to process 5 reports in a day, you're stuck after file #2. Also, the free tier uses GPT-3.5, which struggles with complex multi-page reasoning compared to GPT-4.

Best for: Professionals who read 1-2 PDFs daily, students analyzing papers.

ChatDOC: Highest Question Limit for Short Docs

ChatDOC

Free

Free tier: 2 files/day, 20 pages per file, 200 questions/day. OCR support for scanned PDFs.

Paid plan: Plus at ~$8.99/month, 300 pages per file.

Model: GPT series.

ChatDOC's free tier has a notable limitation: only 20 pages per file. Anything longer than 20 pages can't be fully analyzed on the free plan. But it compensates with 200 questions per day — 4x ChatPDF's per-file limit.

It also supports OCR for scanned documents. I tested it on a scanned contract — accuracy was around 85% for English, noticeably lower for Chinese scans.

Best for: Short documents (contracts, resumes, briefs) that need lots of follow-up questions.

Humata: Clean Interface, Smallest Free Tier

Humata

Free

Free tier: 60 pages total, 10 answers. One-time allowance, no daily reset.

Paid plan: Expert at $9.99/month for 500 pages, $0.02 per additional page.

Model: Not disclosed.

Humata's free tier is the smallest of the five: 60 pages and 10 answers total. That's not a daily limit — it's a lifetime free allowance. Once you use those 10 answers, you need to pay.

The interface is excellent though — clean, fast, with clear citation highlights. And the Expert plan at $9.99/month is reasonably priced compared to competitors.

Best for: One-time use to try the product. Not recommended for ongoing free use.

NotebookLM: The Only Truly Free Option

Google NotebookLM

Completely Free

Free tier: Up to 50 sources per notebook (PDFs, websites, YouTube videos, Google Docs). No apparent question limits.

Paid plan: None. Completely free.

Model: Google Gemini.

NotebookLM is the only tool on this list that's completely free with no meaningful limits. It's not just PDF chat — you can add PDFs, web URLs, YouTube videos, and Google Docs to a single "notebook" and ask cross-source questions.

My favorite feature: Audio Overview. NotebookLM generates a podcast-style audio summary of your documents — two AI hosts discussing the key points. Perfect for consuming a 50-page report during your commute.

The catch: NotebookLM works best for research workflows (multiple sources, cross-referencing). If you just need to quickly extract one number from a single PDF, ChatPDF is faster and more direct.

Best for: Academic research, cross-document analysis, audio summaries.

DocGPT: Cheapest GPT-4 Access

DocGPT

Free Trial

Free tier: Free trial with GPT-3.5, limited features.

Paid plan: $4.99/month for unlimited GPT-4 access + multi-document chat.

Model: GPT-3.5 (free) / GPT-4 (paid).

DocGPT's free trial uses GPT-3.5, similar to ChatPDF. But its paid plan at $4.99/month is the cheapest way to get GPT-4 for PDF analysis — ChatPDF Plus costs $15/month for the same model.

The paid plan also supports multi-document conversations, letting you compare contracts or cross-reference reports in a single chat.

Best for: Users willing to pay a small amount for GPT-4 quality.

Which One Should You Pick?

  • Daily 1-2 PDFs, zero budget: ChatPDF — 50 questions per file is enough for most use cases
  • Many questions on short docs: ChatDOC — 200 questions/day is the highest free limit
  • Research and cross-document analysis: NotebookLM — completely free, most powerful
  • One-time quick use: Humata — 60 pages is enough to test the product
  • GPT-4 on a budget: DocGPT — $4.99/month beats ChatPDF's $15

Money-saving tip: Register for ChatPDF, ChatDOC, and Humata simultaneously. Combined, you get 6 free file uploads per day — enough for most workflows without paying a cent.

Common Limitations Across All Free Tiers

  • Privacy: Most tools store your PDFs on their servers for 7-30 days. For sensitive documents, use NotebookLM or self-hosted alternatives
  • Chinese PDFs: OCR accuracy for Chinese scanned documents is lower than English across all tools
  • Long documents: Free-tier models (GPT-3.5) lose comprehension quality on documents beyond 50 pages
  • Images and tables: Most tools struggle with complex tables and embedded images in PDFs

❓ FAQ

Is ChatPDF free?
Yes. ChatPDF's free plan lets you upload 2 PDF files per day, each up to 120 pages, with 50 questions per file. Resets daily.
What is the best free AI PDF chat tool?
Google NotebookLM is the best completely free option — no usage limits, supports up to 50 sources per notebook, powered by Gemini. For quick daily use, ChatPDF (2 files/day) is the most popular free choice.
How many pages can ChatPDF handle for free?
ChatPDF free tier supports up to 120 pages per file, with 50 questions per file. You can upload 2 files per day.
Is Humata AI free?
Humata offers 60 pages and 10 answers for free. After that, the Expert plan costs $9.99/month for 500 pages.
Can AI PDF tools read Chinese PDFs?
Most tools support Chinese PDFs, but OCR accuracy for Chinese scanned documents is lower than English. NotebookLM and DocGPT have better Chinese support.
Are free AI PDF tools safe for sensitive documents?
Most free tools store your PDF on their servers for 7-30 days. For sensitive documents, use NotebookLM (Google's privacy protections) or self-hosted open-source alternatives.
