Sources: Tencent Marvis official site (marvis.qq.com), Sogou WeChat search (8 articles), tech media reports. Data as of May 21, 2026.

Quick Answer

Tencent built its own Jarvis — and it's free. Marvis launched globally on May 21 across Windows, Mac, and Android. No invite codes required — install and use immediately. You get 10 million free tokens daily, powered by DeepSeek V4 and Tencent's Hunyuan model.

This isn't a beta, not a limited release — it's a full public launch.

What Exactly Is Marvis

In short: an AI assistant that lives on your computer or phone. Not a browser extension, not a web chat widget — it's a system-level Agent that can directly operate your computer.

Built by Tencent's AppBao team — the same team that's been doing PC software distribution for over a decade. They understand the Windows desktop ecosystem better than anyone, and they've channeled years of accumulated capability into Marvis.

At least eight WeChat articles covered the same angle: install it and get six AI agents working for you. While that's slightly clickbaity, it's essentially true — Marvis includes multiple specialized agents, not just a single chatbot.

Core Feature Breakdown

Two Modes: Privacy or Efficiency

Marvis offers two operating modes:

Efficiency Mode: Cloud-device collaboration using Tencent's cloud models. Fast processing, strong capabilities. Best for most scenarios.

Privacy Mode: Runs entirely on local models with zero file uploads. Your documents, images, and contracts never leave your computer. Best for privacy-sensitive users.

This is a smart design choice. Many people avoid AI assistants because they fear file uploads — Privacy Mode eliminates that concern entirely.

Remote Phone-to-PC Control

Once your phone is connected to your PC, you can see real-time task execution and take over control anytime. Your computer becomes portable — let AI handle PC tasks while you're away.

This feature is a game-changer for frequent travelers. Need to edit a file or look something up while out? Marvis handles it remotely.

Intelligent File Search

Not just filename search — Marvis understands file content. Search for people in images, keywords in documents, or organize files by topic. Tencent calls it the "AI Photo Library" and "AI Document Library."

Essentially, it adds an AI comprehension layer to your local files. Thousands of photos and hundreds of documents — Marvis finds exactly what you need.

System Settings by Voice Command

"Set screen brightness to 50%," "Disable startup program XX," "Check current memory usage" — tasks that used to require digging through settings menus now take one sentence.

It deeply understands your OS and hardware — not simple shortcuts, but a genuine system-level Agent.

Document Understanding and Generation

Content optimization, chart generation, copywriting, format conversion — all possible. It's like embedding ChatGPT's file analysis capability directly into your desktop.

Built-in Agent Skill Packs

Marvis comes with several specialized agents pre-installed:

Celebrity Fan Assistant: Search celebrity news, auto-check-in on Weibo, organize HD media. Essential for fan communities.

Gaming Companion: Time-limited offer monitoring, smart action planning, automated daily tasks. A gamer's deal tracker.

Intelligence Monitor: Big tech company intel, social news alerts, ticket information collection. Useful for content creators and information advantage seekers.

Knowledge Manager: Book distillation, personal note summarization, job preparation. Great for learning and career prep.

Office Helper: File format conversion, contract review, operations data analysis. The Swiss Army knife for office work.

PC Manager: One-sentence system settings changes, system cleanup, network repair. System maintenance made simple.

Growth Accelerator: English learning companion, literature organization, AI tool guides.

Lifestyle Curator: Movie recommendations, baby photo organization, travel planning.

These skills cover the major everyday computer use scenarios. They're not generic "do-everything" agents — they're specialized for specific tasks.

Models and Free Credits

According to the official site and tech media reports, Marvis uses these underlying models:

DeepSeek V4: Top-tier Chinese open-source model with strong coding and reasoning capabilities.

Tencent Hunyuan 3 (HY3): Tencent's own large language model with solid Chinese language understanding.

Local Model (Privacy Mode): Specific model not disclosed, but supports on-device inference.

Multiple media outlets report 10 million free tokens daily. That's more than enough for typical daily use — it translates to roughly 5,000 rounds of conversation per day.

System Requirements

Windows: 16GB RAM minimum, Windows 10 or later.

Mac: M1 chip or later, macOS 12+.

Android: Available now via app stores.

iOS: Expected mid-June 2026.

How to Get Started

  1. Visit marvis.qq.com
  2. Download the version for your platform
  3. Install and log in with your WeChat account
  4. Choose Privacy or Efficiency mode
  5. Start using — 10 million tokens available immediately

Who Should Use Marvis

Great for:

  • Users who want a free, capable AI assistant
  • Privacy-conscious users (Privacy Mode has zero uploads)
  • People who want system-level automation, not just chat
  • Windows/Mac/Android users (iOS coming soon)

Probably not for:

  • Users with less than 16GB RAM
  • Those who need advanced reasoning models (limited to DeepSeek V4 and Hunyuan)
