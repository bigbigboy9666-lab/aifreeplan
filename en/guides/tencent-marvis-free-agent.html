Tencent Marvis Launches Free: 10M Tokens Daily, Windows/Mac/Android

In a Nutshell

Tencent has built "Jarvis," and it's free. Marvis was fully launched in the early hours of May 21st, available simultaneously on Windows, Mac, and Android. No invitation codes needed—just install and use. It comes with a daily free allowance of 10 million tokens, powered by DeepSeek V4 and Tencent's own Hunyuan large model.

You read that correctly—not a beta, not limited availability, but a full, official launch.

---

What Exactly is Marvis?

Simply put: It's an AI assistant that lives inside your computer/phone. Not a browser plugin, not a web chat box, but a system-level Agent that can directly operate your computer.

It's developed by Tencent's MyApp (Yingyongbao) team. Yes, the team that has been distributing PC software for over a decade. They understand the Windows desktop ecosystem best, and this time, they've packed years of accumulated capabilities into Marvis.

According to search results, at least 8 WeChat articles highlight the same point: Once installed, you get 6 AI "workhorses" to help you get things done. While the headline might be a bit clickbaity, it's true—Marvis comes with several specialized Agents built-in, not just a single chatbot.

---

Breakdown of Core Features

Two Modes: Choose Between Privacy and Efficiency

Marvis offers two operating modes:

Efficiency Mode: Uses cloud-device collaboration, leveraging Tencent's cloud-based large models for fast, powerful processing. Suitable for most scenarios.

Privacy Mode: Operates entirely using a local large model, with zero file uploads. Your documents, images, contracts, and other files never leave your computer. Ideal for privacy-sensitive users.

This is a clever design. Many people avoid AI assistants due to fears of file uploads; the Privacy Mode directly eliminates this concern.

Remote Phone Control of Your Computer

Once your phone is connected to your computer, you can see the task execution screen in real-time and take over control at any time. Essentially, your computer becomes portable—allowing the AI to handle computer tasks on your behalf even when you're out and about.

This feature is incredibly valuable for frequent business travelers. Need to edit a document or check something urgently while out? Previously, you'd need a remote desktop; now, Marvis can handle it for you directly.

Intelligent File Search

This isn't simple filename search. It understands file content—searching for people in images, keywords in documents, or organizing by topic. The official terms are "AI Photo Library" and "AI Document Library."

In essence, it adds a layer of AI comprehension to your local files. It can help you find the exact item you need among thousands of photos or hundreds of documents on your computer.

Change Computer Settings with a Single Command

"Set my screen brightness to 50%," "Turn off XX software that launches at startup," "Check my current memory usage"—operations that used to require digging through settings can now be done with a simple phrase.

It deeply understands the operating system and hardware information. It's not just simple shortcut commands; it's a true Agent capable of operating the system.

Deep File Understanding and Generation

It can optimize document content, generate charts, refine copy, convert formats—and more. Essentially, it embeds ChatGPT's file analysis capabilities directly into your desktop.

Built-in Agent Skill Packages

Marvis comes with several specialized Agents ready to use after installation, with no configuration required:

  • Celebrity Follower: Searches for celebrity news, auto-signs into Weibo, and organizes HD media materials. A must-have for fan communities.
  • Gaming Companion: Monitors limited-time offers, creates smart action plans, and automates daily tasks. A benefit reminder tool for gamers.
  • Intelligence Monitor: Tracks tech company intelligence, pushes social news, and gathers ticketing information. Useful for self-media or anyone needing an information edge.
  • Knowledge Manager: Distills professional books, extracts personal notes, and prepares job application materials. For learning and job-seeking scenarios.
  • Office Assistant: Converts file formats, reviews contract information, and analyzes operational data. A versatile tool for office scenarios.
  • Computer Steward: Changes system settings via commands, performs system cleanup, and fixes network issues. For system maintenance.
  • Growth Accelerator: Provides English learning companionship, organizes literature, and offers guides for using AI tools.
  • Life Artist: Recommends high-rated movies, helps manage baby growth photos, and evaluates travel plans.

    • These skills cover the main scenarios of daily computer use. They aren't the jack-of-all-trades, master-of-none type, but rather specialized Agents tailored to specific scenarios.

    Models and Free Quota

    According to the official website and tech media reports, the models used by Marvis include:

  • DeepSeek V4: A top-tier domestic open-source model with strong capabilities in both code and reasoning.
  • Tencent Hunyuan3 (Hunyuan3/HY3): Tencent's own large model, with online Chinese language understanding and generation capabilities.
  • Local Model (Privacy Mode): The specific model is undisclosed, but it supports on-device execution.

    • Regarding the free quota, multiple media reports mention 10 million tokens per day. This amount is more than sufficient for daily use—you could have 100 rounds of conversation with the AI in a day, each with 2,000 characters, and it would only use about 2 million tokens.

    Furthermore, this quota resets daily; it's not a one-time allocation. It's available for use every day.

How to Use After Installation

After installing and opening Marvis, it will automatically scan your system environment and then enter the main interface.

1. First Step - Choose a Mode: If you handle many sensitive files, select Privacy Mode; if you prioritize efficiency, select Efficiency Mode.

2. Second Step - Connect Your Phone (Optional): After installing Marvis on your phone, scan the QR code to pair it, enabling remote control of your computer.

3. Third Step - Use Natural Language Commands. No need to learn command syntax; just tell it what you want to do, as if you were chatting.
