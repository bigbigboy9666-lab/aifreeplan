Suno AI Music Generator Free: 10 Songs Per Day, No Credit Card Needed

Suno is the hottest AI music generator right now. Type lyrics or a description, and get a complete song with melody, vocals, and production — not just a backing track. The free plan gives you 10 songs per day with no credit card required.

Suno Free Tier: What You Actually Get

The free plan is straightforward:

  • 10 songs per day (each song is 1-2 minutes long)
  • No credit card required
  • Custom lyrics supported
  • Instrumental mode available (no vocals)
  • Free songs can be shared publicly
  • Commercial use requires a paid plan

    • The daily limit resets at UTC midnight (around 4 PM Beijing time). Each generation produces two variations — you pick the one you like better.

    300 songs per month for free is generous compared to most AI tools. That's enough to experiment daily without worrying about running out.

    Suno Pricing: Pro and Premier Plans

    If the free tier isn't enough:

    Pro Plan — $10/month (or $96/year, 20% off):

  • 500 songs per month
  • Latest v5 model access
  • Commercial rights
  • Song Editor
  • Stem separation
  • Audio uploads up to 8 minutes

    • Premier Plan — $30/month (or $288/year, 20% off):

  • 2,000 songs per month
  • Suno Studio multitrack editor
  • MIDI export
  • Persona voices
  • Early access to new features

    • For most hobbyists, the free plan is plenty. The Pro plan makes sense if you're releasing music commercially.

    How to Use Suno: Step-by-Step

    1. Go to suno.com and sign in with Google or Microsoft

    2. Choose "Custom" to write your own lyrics, or describe the style you want

    3. Enable "Instrumental" for music without vocals

    4. Click "Create" and wait ~30 seconds

    Pro tips for better results:

  • Write prompts in English — Suno's training data is mostly English music, so English descriptions produce more natural melodies
  • Use section labels like `[Verse]`, `[Chorus]`, `[Bridge]` in custom lyrics to control song structure
  • Be specific about genre: "1980s synth rock with distorted guitars and drum machine" works better than just "rock"
  • Use the free plan to test styles first, then batch-generate with a paid plan
