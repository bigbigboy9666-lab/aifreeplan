Gemini 3.5 Flash Free Access: 5 Ways to Use Google's Latest Model

Google just launched Gemini 3.5 Flash at I/O 2026 — and it's now the default model in the Gemini app. The headline: it beats the previous flagship Gemini 3.1 Pro on coding and agent tasks, runs 4x faster, and costs 50% less.

The best part? Free users get access too.

Here are five ways to use Gemini 3.5 Flash, ranked by practicality.

Method 1: Google AI Studio (Recommended)

URL: aistudio.google.com

If I could only recommend one method, this is it. AI Studio is technically for developers, but regular users can treat it as a chatbot. Sign up with a Google account, switch to Gemini 3.5 Flash in the model selector (top right), and start chatting.

Why it's the best: Free credits are generous. For learning, testing, and small projects, the free tier covers most needs.

Limitations: Requires VPN access. No Nano Banana Pro image generation, Veo video, or other premium features. Free credits have a daily cap — once hit, you wait for refresh or pay.

API pricing (if you exceed free tier): Input $1.5/million tokens, output $9/million tokens. Significantly cheaper than 3.1 Pro.

Method 2: Gemini Website (Full Feature Set)

URL: gemini.google.com

Good news: 3.5 Flash is now the default model for all users, including free accounts. Open Gemini and you're already using it — no model switching needed.

What's unique about the website: Full access to Nano Banana Pro, Veo 3.1, NotebookLM integration, music generation, and other premium features. The new "Neural Expressive" interface auto-highlights key points, inserts images, and shows interactive visualizations.

Free tier limits: Usage is capped per 5-hour window based on compute units. When you hit the limit, the system auto-downgrades to Flash-Lite instead of disconnecting — you keep chatting, just with a lighter model.

Method 3: 2233.ai (China Direct Access)

URL: 2233.ai

For users in China who want zero friction. No VPN required, direct domestic access, smooth performance. The interface mirrors Google's official design — minimal learning curve.

Gemini 3.5 Flash is already available on the platform. Supports WeChat payment, with pricing lower than official subscriptions. Flexible day or month plans.

Best for: Users who prioritize convenience over cost optimization.

Method 4: Poe (Multi-Model Comparison)

URL: poe.com

Poe bundles GPT, Claude, Gemini, and DeepSeek into one subscription. 3.5 Flash is live on the platform.

Strength: Compare different models side-by-side in one interface. No need to jump between platforms when evaluating response quality.

Free tier: Daily free usage varies by model. Paid subscription: $20/month.

Trade-off: As a middleman, performance doesn't match the official site. Requires VPN.

Method 5: GPTSAPI (Developer Integration)

URL: gptsapi.net

For developers who want to integrate Gemini into their workflows — Cursor, Cherry Studio, Chatbox, and similar clients.

A well-regarded API relay platform in the Chinese developer community. Supports OpenAI-compatible format. GPT, Claude, and Gemini models available through one API key.

Pricing: Pay-per-use, matching Google's official rates. No subscription required.

Setup: Takes minutes. Change the endpoint URL, paste your API key, and use Gemini in your existing tools.

3.5 Flash vs 3.1 Pro: Which Should You Choose?

Choose 3.5 Flash when:

  • Writing code or debugging
  • Running agent tasks and tool calls
  • Daily conversations and quick questions
  • Any scenario where speed and cost matter

    • Choose 3.1 Pro when:

  • Deep research and academic analysis
  • Complex reasoning tasks
  • Scenarios requiring the highest accuracy

Google's own data confirms this: on the Human Last Exam (HLE) and ARC-AGI-2 benchmarks, 3.1 Pro outperforms 3.5 Flash. The trade-off is speed and cost — 3.5 Flash delivers 4x faster responses at half the price.

For most developers, 3.5 Flash is the better daily driver. Reserve 3.1 Pro for tasks where reasoning depth is non-negotiable.

Free Tier: How Long Do Credits Last?

Google AI Studio: Free credits handle light usage (dozens of conversations daily) comfortably. Heavy users should monitor the $1.5/$9 per million token pricing.

Gemini Website: Free users get compute-based limits refreshed every 5 hours. The auto-downgrade to Flash-Lite means you're never fully locked out.

2233.ai: Day/month purchase plans offer predictable costs without worrying about credit refreshes.

Bottom Line

Gemini 3.5 Flash represents a significant leap — coding capability that surpasses the previous flagship, at 4x the speed and half the price. Free access makes it the most accessible high-performance AI model available today.

Pick the method that matches your technical comfort level and location. For most people, AI Studio offers the best free experience. For China users, 2233.ai removes all friction. For developers, GPTSAPI integrates directly into existing workflows.

The model is here. The free tier is generous. Start building.
