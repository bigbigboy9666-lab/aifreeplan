Why Did Doubao Suddenly Start Charging?

Saying "suddenly" is actually not accurate.

From its launch in 2023 until now, Doubao has relied on subsidies from ByteDance. With 345 million monthly active users and billions of daily dialogue requests, the computational cost is astronomical. At least five articles found via Sogou WeChat search shared the same viewpoint: AI services are different from traditional internet products, as their marginal cost does not approach zero.

Every additional user means additional GPU computational expenses.

ByteDance is not a charity. The essence of Doubao's charging is: the user base has grown, the subsidies are unsustainable, and it's time to cash in.

    Is the Free Version Still Good Enough?

    To be honest: it depends on how you use it.

    If you just use Doubao for chatting, checking the weather, writing social media captions, or translating a few sentences—the free version is perfectly sufficient. The basic Lark model handles these daily tasks without any problem.

    But if your use cases fall into the following scenarios, the free version starts to fall short:

  • Writing long texts, editing papers: The context window is insufficient, leading to frequent "memory lapses."
  • Writing code, debugging: The gap in reasoning ability is noticeable.
  • Complex analysis, multi-step reasoning: The free version will "talk nonsense with a straight face" (this is a direct quote from users).
  • Using during peak hours: Queuing is the norm.

    • A metaphor from a Sogou WeChat search article is very apt: The difference between the free and paid versions is more like the difference between "standard mode" and "high-performance mode." It's not a question of whether you can use it, but whether it works well for you.

How to Decide Whether to Pay

Ask yourself three questions:

1. Do you use Doubao for more than 30 minutes a day? If yes, the efficiency boost from the Pro version might be worth it.

2. What do you use Doubao for? If it's for chat and simple writing, the free version is enough. For heavy use in work or study, consider paying.

3. Do you have alternatives? If DeepSeek or Kimi can meet your needs, there's no reason to pay for Doubao.

My advice: Start with the free version. Only consider paying when you truly feel it's "not enough." Don't impulsively subscribe just because "it might get more expensive later"—the price war for AI tools has only just begun, and the future will bring either cheaper prices or more choices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will the Doubao free version be cut?

A: Not for now. ByteDance's strategy is "basic free + premium paid," not "charge for everything." The user base of 345 million MAUs is Doubao's greatest asset and won't be given up easily.

Q: Is Seedance 2.0 video generation still free?

A: As of May 2026, Doubao's official homepage still promotes "Log in to use Seedance 2.0 for free." However, the duration of this promotional window is uncertain. It's advisable to use it as much as possible while you can.

Q: Which is better, Doubao Pro or ChatGPT Plus?

A: It depends on the scenario. For Chinese dialogue and writing, Doubao Pro has a better grasp of the Chinese context. For English, code, and complex reasoning, ChatGPT Plus is stronger. In terms of price, Doubao Pro is less than half the cost.

Q: Which version should an enterprise user choose?

A: If your team uses Feishu (Lark), Doubao's Business/Enterprise version offers the highest integration. Otherwise, consider Tongyi Qianwen Enterprise or ChatGPT Team.

Q: Will Doubao's quality improve after charging?

A: This is what users care about most and also the biggest uncertainty. Multiple articles via Sogou WeChat search all ask the same question: "What if it's still 'clumsy' after I pay?" There isn't enough user feedback yet to make a judgment. It's recommended to wait a month or two to see actual reviews before deciding.

Q: Will other domestic AI services start charging too?

A: Highly likely. Kimi, Tongyi Qianwen, and Ernie Bot are all testing paid models. The window for free AI is shrinking—take advantage of it while you can.
